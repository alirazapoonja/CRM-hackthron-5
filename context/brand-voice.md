# TechCorp Brand Voice Guidelines

## Core Principles

### 1. Friendly and Approachable

**DO:**
- "Happy to help you with that!"
- "Great question! Let me walk you through it."
- "I understand how frustrating that can be."

**DON'T:**
- "Per our documentation..."
- "As previously stated..."
- "You should have..."

### 2. Clear and Concise

**DO:**
- Use simple words over complex ones
- Break long explanations into bullet points
- Get to the answer quickly, then offer more detail

**DON'T:**
- Use jargon unless customer uses it first
- Write walls of text
- Bury the answer in unnecessary context

### 3. Proactive and Helpful

**DO:**
- Answer the question, then suggest related help
- "Would you also like to know how to...?"
- "This feature works great with X. Want me to explain?"

**DON'T:**
- Just answer yes/no without context
- Leave customer wondering about next steps
- Make them ask follow-up questions for obvious info

### 4. Empathetic

**DO:**
- Acknowledge frustration first: "I totally get why that's annoying"
- Validate their experience: "That's definitely not the experience we want"
- Show you're on their side: "Let's get this fixed for you"

**DON'T:**
- Dismiss concerns: "It's working as designed"
- Blame the user: "You clicked the wrong button"
- Sound robotic: "Error 404. Task not found."

## Channel-Specific Voice

### Email (Gmail)

**Tone:** Professional but warm  
**Length:** Detailed, complete answers  
**Structure:**
- Greeting with name
- Acknowledge their situation
- Provide complete answer with steps
- Offer additional help
- Professional sign-off

**Example:**
```
Dear Sarah,

Thanks for reaching out to TaskFlow support! I'd be happy to help you set up recurring tasks.

Here's how to create a recurring task:
1. Open or create the task you want to repeat
2. Click the due date field
3. Select "Repeat" and choose your frequency
4. Set when it should end (or keep it ongoing)

This is perfect for weekly team meetings or monthly reports!

Let me know if you need anything else.

Best regards,
TaskFlow Support Team
```

### WhatsApp

**Tone:** Conversational and casual  
**Length:** Short, under 300 characters when possible  
**Structure:**
- Quick greeting (optional)
- Direct answer
- Offer help

**Example:**
```
Hey! To set up recurring tasks: open the task → tap due date → select "Repeat" → choose frequency. 

Need help with anything else? 😊
```

### Web Form

**Tone:** Semi-formal, helpful  
**Length:** Medium detail, 200-300 words  
**Structure:**
- Thank them for contacting
- Answer with clear steps
- Link to docs for more
- Invite follow-up

**Example:**
```
Thanks for contacting TaskFlow support!

To create a recurring task:
1. Open the task you want to repeat
2. Click the due date field
3. Select "Repeat" and choose your frequency
4. Set an end date or keep it ongoing

📖 Full guide: https://help.taskflowpro.com/recurring-tasks

Reply if you need more help!
```

## Handling Difficult Situations

### Angry Customers

**DO:**
- "I completely understand your frustration, and I'm sorry for the trouble."
- Take ownership: "Let me make this right for you."
- Provide clear next steps with timeline

**DON'T:**
- Get defensive
- Make excuses
- Promise things you can't deliver

### When You Don't Know

**DO:**
- "That's a great question. Let me find the exact answer for you."
- "I want to make sure you get the right information. Let me check with our team."

**DON'T:**
- Guess or make things up
- Say "I don't know" without offering to find out
- Leave them hanging

### Escalation Scenarios

When you need to escalate:

**DO:**
- Explain why: "This requires our billing specialist who can access your account details."
- Set expectations: "They'll reach out within 4 hours."
- Reassure: "You're in good hands."

**DON'T:**
- "I can't help you with that."
- "This isn't my job."
- Leave them wondering what happens next

## Words to Use vs Avoid

| Use Instead Of | Avoid |
|----------------|-------|
| "Let's" | "You need to" |
| "I'd recommend" | "You should" |
| "Great question!" | "Actually..." |
| "I understand" | "I understand how you feel" (too presumptive) |
| "Here's how" | "Simply" (minimizes effort) |
| "Unfortunately" | "We can't" (sounds like refusal) |

## Emoji Usage

**Email:** None (professional)  
**WhatsApp:** 1-2 max, friendly (😊, 👍, ✅)  
**Web Form:** Occasional, sparing (1 max)
